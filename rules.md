# Trading Rules

> "The elements of good trading are: (1) cutting losses, (2) cutting losses, and (3) cutting losses." — Ed Seykota

---

## TIER 1: THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

These rules cannot be broken. Ever. For any reason.

### R1. Maximum Risk Per Trade: 2% of Account
- $250 account = $5 max risk per trade
- $500 account = $10 max risk per trade
- $1,000 account = $20 max risk per trade
- This determines your stop loss distance. On MNQ: 1 point = $2 per contract.
- $5 risk = 2.5 point stop. $10 risk = 5 point stop. $20 risk = 10 point stop.

### R2. Maximum Daily Loss: 3% of Account
- $250 account = $7.50 max daily loss
- $500 account = $15 max daily loss
- $1,000 account = $30 max daily loss
- When you hit this number, you are DONE for the day. Close the platform. No exceptions.

### R3. Never Move Your Stop Loss Further Away
- Set it before entry. Leave it alone.
- Moving it further = hoping. Hoping = losing.
- You may move it CLOSER (trailing stop) but never further.

### R4. Never Average Down
- If your trade is losing, do NOT add to the position.
- Jesse Livermore: "Never average losses. It is the most asinine play."
- If you're wrong, get out. Don't make it worse.

### R5. Two Strikes Rule
- After 2 consecutive losses that followed your plan, stop trading for the day.
- Your edge hasn't disappeared, but your mental state has degraded.
- Walk away. Come back tomorrow.

### R6. No Trade Without Full System Confirmation
- Bias (4H/1H trend) + Setup (liquidity sweep + BOS) + Confluence + Confirmation
- If any piece is missing, there is no trade. Period.
- "If there is no setup, there is no trade." — TJR

## TIER 2: TIME RULES

### R7. No Trading Before 9:50 AM ET
- The first 20 minutes after open are for price development.
- Watch, don't trade. Let the market show its hand.

### R8. No Trading During Lunch (11:30 AM - 1:30 PM ET)
- Volume drops 40%+. Price chops sideways. Stop-outs increase.
- This is the market's dead zone.

### R9. No Trading After 3:45 PM ET
- Close-of-day volatility is unpredictable.
- Close all positions before this time.

### R10. Best Kill Zones
- **AM Session:** 9:50 - 10:10 AM ET (TJR's primary kill zone)
- **PM Session:** 1:30 - 3:00 PM ET (afternoon continuation)
- Focus your energy on these windows.

### R11. No Trading on Major News Days Without a Plan
- FOMC, NFP, CPI can move MNQ 100+ points in seconds.
- Either have a specific news-trading strategy or sit it out.

## TIER 3: ENTRY RULES

### R12. Trade With the Trend Only
- Check the 1H chart first. Is price above EMA 9 and EMA 21?
- If YES = only look for LONGS today
- If NO = only look for SHORTS today
- If SIDEWAYS = don't trade

### R13. Minimum 2:1 Reward-to-Risk
- If your stop is 10 points ($20), your target must be at least 20 points ($40).
- If you can't find 2:1, the setup isn't good enough. Skip it.

### R14. Wait for Confirmation
- Don't anticipate the move. Wait for it to start, then join.
- Livermore: "To anticipate the market is to gamble. To be patient and react only when the market gives the signal is to speculate."

### R15. One Contract Only (Until Consistently Profitable)
- Do not trade more than 1 MNQ contract until you are profitable for 2+ consecutive weeks.
- Going from 1 to 2 contracts doubles your risk. Earn it.

### R16. Pre-Trade Checklist (Must Complete Before Every Entry)
- [ ] What is the 1H trend direction?
- [ ] Is there a liquidity sweep or key level?
- [ ] Is there a break of structure (BOS)?
- [ ] What is my third confluence? (FVG, order block, EMA, VWAP)
- [ ] Where is my exact entry price?
- [ ] Where is my exact stop loss? How many points?
- [ ] Where is my exact take profit? How many points?
- [ ] Is my risk-to-reward at least 2:1?
- [ ] Am I within a kill zone (time window)?
- [ ] Am I emotionally neutral? (not angry, tired, or desperate)

## TIER 4: EXIT RULES

### R17. Set TP and SL Before Entry
- Both must be defined BEFORE you click buy or sell.
- If you don't know where you're getting out, you don't know what you're doing.

### R18. Never Extend Take Profit
- Don't move your TP further hoping for more. Greed kills accounts.
- Take what the market gives you.

### R19. Close Immediately if Thesis Invalidated
- If price closes past your key level, get out. Don't wait for the stop.
- Paul Tudor Jones: "If I have positions going against me, I get right out."

### R20. Partial Exits Are Allowed
- TP1: Close 50% at first target (e.g., 1:1 R:R)
- TP2: Close remaining 50% at second target (e.g., 2:1 R:R)
- Move stop to breakeven after TP1 hits.

## TIER 5: PSYCHOLOGY RULES

### R21. Mandatory Break After Every Loss
- After any loss: 10-30 minute break. Walk away from the screen.
- Your brain enters fight-or-flight after a loss. Logic is impaired.
- Do NOT make decisions in this state.

### R22. No Revenge Trading
- Never increase position size after a loss.
- Never take an unplanned trade to "get your money back."
- If you feel the urge, that IS the signal to close the platform.

### R23. Process Over Outcomes
- After each trade, ask: "Did I follow my system?"
- If yes and you lost = acceptable. The system has a win rate, not 100%.
- If no and you won = dangerous. You got lucky. That's worse than a disciplined loss.

### R24. Think in Years, Not Days
- You are building a skill. Expecting profits in month 1 is like expecting to dunk after your first basketball practice (TJR).
- One strategy. One system. No switching every week.

### R25. Journal Every Trade
- Entry reason, confluences present, stop placement, take profit, outcome, lesson learned.
- Without a journal, you cannot identify patterns in your mistakes.
- Review weekly. Look for: losing time windows, losing setups, emotional triggers.

### R26. The Sleep Test (Livermore)
- "If you can't sleep at night because of your stock market position, you have gone too far."
- Reduce position size until you can sleep.

## TIER 6: ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

### R27. Maximum 1-2 Trades Per Day
- As a beginner, 1 trade is ideal. 2 is the maximum.
- More trades does NOT equal more money. It usually equals more losses.

### R28. Withdraw Profits
- Livermore's rule: withdraw half of every significant profit.
- Pay yourself. The purpose of trading is to make money you can use.

### R29. Account Scaling Levels
| Level | Account Size | Contracts | When to Advance |
|-------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | $250 - $500 | 1 | Profitable 2+ weeks |
| 2 | $500 - $1,000 | 1-2 | Profitable 2+ weeks at Level 1 |
| 3 | $1,000 - $2,500 | 2-3 | Profitable 1+ month at Level 2 |
| 4 | $2,500+ | 3-5 | Proven track record |

- If you start losing at a new level, drop back immediately.

### R30. The $400 Floor (For $500 Accounts)
- If your account drops below $400 (20% drawdown), stop live trading.
- Go back to paper trading. Rebuild confidence and savings.
- Do not try to trade your way out of a hole with a depleted account.

### Quick Risk Calculator
```
Risk per trade = Account Size x 0.02 (2%)
Max stop in points = Risk per trade / $2
Example: $500 x 0.02 = $10 / $2 = 5 point stop maximum
```
